# 📐 Laser Cutting Files

This folder contains the 2D CAD files (DXF format) designed for fabrication using a laser cutter, waterjet, or CNC router. These parts make up the rigid planar structural elements of the RC car chassis.

## 📂 File Directory

* **[Base.dxf](file:///d:/DIY%20Fast%20RC%20Car%20Project/Laser%20Cutting%20Files/Base.dxf)**:
  * The main chassis bottom deck. It is the structural backbone of the entire car. It features pre-aligned mounting holes for:
    * Rear gearbox housing.
    * Front steering wing mounts.
    * Steering servo bracket.
    * Battery tray and ESC/Receiver positioning.
* **[Steering.dxf](file:///d:/DIY%20Fast%20RC%20Car%20Project/Laser%20Cutting%20Files/Steering.dxf)**:
  * The top deck or steering plate. It mounts above the steering knuckles, adding structural reinforcement to the front end and anchoring the steering linkage pivots.

## 🛠️ Fabrication Specifications

### 1. Materials Selection
Choosing the right material for the chassis is crucial for performance:
* **Carbon Fiber (Recommended)**: 2.0mm to 3.0mm thickness. Extremely stiff, lightweight, and professional. Perfect for high-speed builds.
* **Delrin (POM)**: 3.0mm thickness. Highly durable, self-lubricating, and has excellent impact absorption.
* **Acrylic (For Prototyping)**: 3.0mm thickness. Easy and cheap to cut, but brittle and will likely crack during high-speed crashes.
* **Aluminum (6061-T6)**: 2.5mm or 3.0mm thickness. Strong and durable, but adds extra weight.
* **Plywood (MDF / Birch)**: 3.0mm thickness. Great for quick visual fit-checks.

### 2. Laser Cutting Options
* **Units**: All DXF files are exported in **millimeters (mm)**. Please ensure your laser/CNC controller software import settings are configured to millimeters to maintain 1:1 scale.
* **Kerf Compensation**: A laser cut typically burns away ~0.1mm - 0.2mm of material (the kerf). For precise fitment of screws and bearings, ensure your cutting service accounts for kerf or test fit a scrap piece first.
